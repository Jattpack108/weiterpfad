# 08 — COPY, CONTENT AND TONE

## Tone profile
The copy should feel:
- clear
- intelligent
- restrained
- humane
- calm
- serious

It should not feel:
- sentimental
- fluffy
- bureaucratic
- generic startup
- over-marketed
- inflated

## Writing rules
- short to medium sentences
- clear labels
- low jargon
- no empty hype
- no filler paragraphs
- no false urgency
- no exaggerated claims

## Good examples
- A better way to find the next place after school.
- One structured path for work, living, day programs and counselling.
- Designed for families who need clarity before they need more calls and more uncertainty.

## Bad examples
- Revolutionising support through cutting-edge AI.
- Empowering the ecosystem through next-generation transformation.
- Seamlessly unlocking synergies across stakeholders.

## Placeholder content rule
The site may use tasteful placeholder text.
It must always sound plausible for this product and this year.
No lorem ipsum. No fake testimonials. No invented institutional promises.

## Page emphasis
### Homepage
Strong thesis, matching visibility, trust.

### About
Problem clarity and strategic reason for existence.

### Product
Mechanics and staged roadmap.

### Providers
Participation logic and trust.

### For families
Human clarity and practical orientation.

### Contact
Simple, credible conversion.

## Emotional tone rule
Warmth through clarity, not through sentimentality.
